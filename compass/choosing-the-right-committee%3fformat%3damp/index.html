The Onus of Choice - A Guide to Selecting Committees

As you are introduced to the committees at NorthMUN 2021 for the first time, you may hesitate on what to choose. If you are a first-time delegate, your first instinct may be to choose a General Assembly that seems the least imposing. If you have some experience, the preference may be a committee you have already delegated in. What unites both these emotion-driven responses is a fear of making wrong choices, and one, in extreme cases, coined by Princeton University philosopher Walter Kaufmann: decidophobia.

Let’s take a step back. What is the purpose of MUN? To me, it is a platform to upgrade my communication skillset, absorbing new skills and refining pre-existing ones. No matter my future profession or current passions, these skills, notably those of debate, negotiation, and rhetoric, may be altered and applied in any circumstance. Thus, we at NorthMUN have sought to foster a welcoming environment in each of our committees for all delegates to experiment.

More practically, choosing a committee first involves determining your areas of interest. You may particularly enjoy economics, political dynamics, or health systems. Perhaps, contemporary issues are not your dish, and historical scenarios are your preference. Mayhap, you are looking for a committee that aids you in enhancing your adaptation abilities more than research skills. 

Luckily, NorthMUN 2021 has created a committees program that offers all of these experiences. Nonetheless, having topics that interest you does not automatically imply that you will do well. Your success will depend on the effort you put in; spending an hour writing a position paper can make all the difference between ‘standing aloof in giant ignorance’ and leading debate. Under our committees page, I strongly recommend you to browse through each of our topic overviews and committee descriptions to get a better grasp of whether or not a certain committee fits your tastes. However, we also encourage you to explore new fields and venture beyond your comfort zone. In line with the mere-exposure effect, your interest in a particular field is a function of time and experience, so do not bar yourself from endless possibilities.

Lastly, please use the background guides written by your respective Directors to your greatest advantage as they will serve as the initiating force of your own research. Should any questions regarding our topics or its selection process arise, please do not hesitate email me at cos@northmun.com. I wish you all the best in your preparations and look forward to meeting you virtually!
