The United Nations

Before we dive into Model United Nations, it is important that you understand the actual United Nations. Commonly abbreviated as the UN, the United Nations was founded in 1945 in the aftermath of the Second World War. An intergovernmental organisation succeeding the previous League of Nations, the UN was created with the primary purpose of maintaining international peace and security. Nowadays, the UN is also heavily involved with maintaining the global ecosystem surrounding human rights, environmental conservation, and sustainable development. Programs such as the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) as well as numerous other initiatives are clear examples of their mission in action.

Much of the UN’s substantive work is accomplished through events called conferences. During these events, ambassadors representing nations from all around the world congregate to resolve issues of various scales, ranging from international nuclear disarmament and resolving regional conflicts to combating global warming and ending poverty. These conferences can last up to seven days, and, along with their various subcommittees meeting year-round, the UN is able to provide the world with a safe and effective platform for dialogue and, ultimately, change.

It is important to note that the UN is not where national laws are created. Its decisions cannot be enforced on member states as the respect of sovereignty is established within the UN charter; domestic constitutions overpower these resolutions.

Model United Nations

With a considerable number of participants from all over the world, Model United Nations (MUN) is amongst the most well-known activities for high school students and young adults. In MUN, participants — also called delegates — assume the job of ambassadors who speak on behalf of one of the 193 Member States of the United Nations. Since delegates are assigned to represent a specific country, they do not advocate for their own convictions on the topic at hand; rather, they abide to their nation’s stance during the conference and attempt to mimic the actions their country would take when debating topics.

Partaking in MUN will enable delegates to garner significant debating abilities such as public-speaking, teamwork, and leadership with real-world applications. MUN also grants delegates the chance to interact with individuals from various nations and cultures, thus, expanding their common point of view. Schools and colleges realise that Model United Nations trains these aptitudes, so cooperation in MUN can likewise be a foundation of a formidable university application, particularly for those who attain executive positions in conferences and school clubs.

What to Expect at a Traditional MUN Conference

With the multitude of MUN conferences being held worldwide, there are a myriad of distinct styles and qualities that every conference possess as well as a number of unique variations that make each one one of a kind. At NorthMUN, we are centred around the notion of fostering the spirit of the UN, striving to be as realistic as possible in order to provide delegates with a fulfilling, substantial, and affordable experience.

Many key components of MUN, however, remain consistent throughout all conferences and are highly dependent on the delegates. First, a delegate should always read his/her committee background guide thoroughly to understand the stance of the country he/she represents. Upon the reading of the backgrounder and conducting further research, delegates should also submit a position paper which details an overview of the topic(s), his/her position, and potential solutions.

Throughout the conference, delegates must always stay sharp and be prepared to think spontaneously. There will be a wide range of topics that will be explored; thus, you must be prepared to present your stance at all times. Towards the end of the day, delegates must come together to agree on a concluding idea and draft a resolution paper. The resolution paper is the key to the end of a successful conference as it includes the final proposal of the committee as a whole after compromising. Awards for each committee, specifically Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, Honourable Mention(s), Best Researched Delegate, and Best New Delegate, are presented at the end of the conference. Keep in mind the number of awards is subject to change based on the size of the committee. In the end, however, this conference will gift you only what you put into it. If you actively involve yourself in the debate and come prepared, your dais will notice, and you will reap the benefits of your efforts.
