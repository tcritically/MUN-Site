The Drive Behind Success - Mock Sessions

Model United Nations is highly favoured and accessible by many students and schools in Vancouver. In order to support students’ growth in Model UN, many schools provide extensive programs for students to get familiar with, learn about and try out this fascinating type of debate. By educating students about current global issues, how to analyse a topic through various aspects and the formality of committee sessions, students can be well-prepared and know what to do at their first MUN conference. However, Model UN clubs at school do not only offer direct education and knowledge, but also a very essential aspect — Mock Model United Nations sessions.

Since a MUN committee contains various tasks and procedures, it definitely requires understanding and experience. This creates a scenario where beginner delegates are extremely confused about what is happening during committee sessions. Therefore, by adding in a mock Model United Nations session at school, delegates can be able to get a glimpse at how committees are going to run before being in an actual conference. The structure of a mock MUN session is similar with a committee session, except for the fact that teachers or student leaders will constantly be guiding delegates throughout and assisting delegates to familiarise the rules of procedure of MUN. This does not only offer more experience to students but also enhance their confidence to speak up when debating in a committee. It is believed that these mock MUN sessions can also lead to further education: Teachers or student leaders tend to spark discussion by brainstorming and mind-mapping the topic with students before starting the session, encouraging students to gain more global knowledge and learn how to look at topics in different perspectives and ideas. What if you are already experienced? Mock MUN sessions can be able to track your progress, such as your fluency in speeches and how often you contribute to the committee.

NorthMUN 2021 views the importance of what schools can contribute to each delegate’s Model United Nations development and we believe that school clubs can definitely reflect delegates’ Model UN career. As an active member of the MUN community, I have come across some delegates who were unfortunate to lack a decent MUN club at school. Therefore, I highly encourage these delegates to take the initiative to start a club and experience the benefits of mock MUN sessions to themselves as well as their fellow classmates. If you have inquiries about school MUN clubs and mock sessions, please do not hesitate to contact me, the USG of Committees at committees@northmun.com.
