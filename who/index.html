World Health Organisation

 
 
 

A General Assembly of the United Nations since 1948, the World Health Organisation (WHO) is constituted by 194 member states and over 7000 officials. Meeting annually at the World Health Assembly in Geneva, this UN body invests in topics primarily regarding worldwide medical care and public health emergencies. Through its work, WHO has eradicated smallpox, limited the spread of HIV, and improved access to clean drinking water. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the World Health Organisation has been a leading force, uniting countries in a worldwide response plan, supplying countries with essential goods and workers, as well as ensuring the accuracy and transparency of data.

WHO is a committee suited for beginner delegates but is open to delegates of all experience levels. Delegates who wish to receive feedback for their position papers and/or be eligible for the Best Researched Delegate award must submit them by 12 May 2021 at 11.59pm PDT and. Delegates who wish to be considered for any committee awards (Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, and Honourable Mention) must submit their position papers by 16 May 2021 at 11.59pm PDT to who@northmun.com. 

Outbreak Management in Developing Nations’ Crises

The onset of SARS-COV2 has hindered the World Health Organization's progress towards achieving the Sustainable Development Target 3.3: “[combatting] hepatitis, water-borne diseases and other communicable diseases” by 2030. Humanitarian emergencies in these countries further complicate the system, inflicting additional pressure on already fragile health infrastructure. With the recent humanitarian crisis surges and perspectives from the pandemic, preventing and managing procedures may be outdated. The world’s largest refugee camp, Cox’s Bazar, and the Kerala Floods in India have both experienced fatal outbreaks. Some of the initiatives proposed at the previous World Health Assembly in 2019 are partnerships with local organisations, laboratory testing, and funding, among others. Especially amidst these anomalous times, finding more effective outbreak management measures is of utmost importance.
